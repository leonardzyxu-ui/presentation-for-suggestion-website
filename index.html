Executive Summary: Project Rationale

The STUCO Feedback Portal addresses the need for a safe, confidential, and actionable channel for student-teacher communication. It aims to formalize the feedback loop, moving beyond anecdotal comments to provide constructive, summarized data for continuous improvement.

  • Core Goal: Balance Anonymity and Accountability
    • Students provide feedback anonymously, but inappropriate content is flagged and traced by STUCO moderation.
    • The system summarizes qualitative feedback into positive, actionable themes for teachers.
  • Expected Impact
    • Increase in quality and volume of student input.
    • Empower teachers with data-driven insights on classroom effectiveness.
    • Foster a culture of open communication and trust.

Phase 1: Immediate Pilot (Microsoft Forms)

An immediate, low-resource deployment targeting Years 6-8 to test the concept and gather initial data.

Scope & Tools

  • Tool: Microsoft Forms (Existing School License).
  • Target Audience: Students in Years 6, 7, and 8.
  • Data Collection: Focused on core teaching suggestions only.

Action Plan

  • STUCO creates form with branching logic for teacher selection.
  • Launch next week via tutor group announcements.
  • STUCO manually monitors and anonymizes data before summary delivery.

Phase 2: Custom Web Portal (Full Features)

Implementation of the full-featured, secure web application, offering advanced functionality for teachers, students, and administration.

Student Experience

  • Secure login via Microsoft (SSO).
  • Categorized feedback options (Teacher, Policy, Facility, Other).
  • Flexible anonymity option with traceability for inappropriate content.

Teacher Dashboard

  • Real-time, graphical summary of feedback trends (e.g., Radar Chart).
  • No direct access to raw, identifiable student comments.
  • Mechanism to request clarification via STUCO intermediary.

Administrative & Security

  • Secure Microsoft Azure integration (Client ID/Secret required).
  • Automated safeguarding triggers for high-risk reports.
  • STUCO moderation panel for content screening.

Teacher Dashboard: AI Feedback Summary

The core feature for teachers: a positive, moderated summary of constructive feedback generated by the system's analysis, focusing on key strengths and growth opportunities.

Key Strengths (17 Positive Reports)

  • Clarity of Explanations: Students frequently highlight how your use of real-world examples and visual aids makes complex topics easy to grasp.
  • Supportive Environment: The vast majority of feedback praises the welcoming and non-judgemental atmosphere you cultivate, encouraging questions.
  • Fairness: No negative reports regarding fairness; consistent positive remarks on assignment grading and classroom management rules.

Growth Opportunities (5 Actionable Suggestions)

  • Pacing of Lessons: Several students suggested slowing down the introduction of new concepts, particularly on Fridays.
  • Resource Availability: There were requests for digital copies of worksheets or notes to be uploaded to the school portal before the lesson.
  • Group Work: Feedback suggested more variety in grouping strategies to avoid working with the same peers every time.
